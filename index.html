Research Interests

Broadly, I am interested in:

  • Generative models & LLMs — training and sampling on discrete spaces, controllable generation, length and structure control, and improving the faithfulness and efficiency of large language models.
  • Reinforcement learning — RL for long-horizon reasoning and planning, RL from human feedback, and combining RL with generative models.
  • Embodied intelligence — language-conditioned control, agents that can act in the physical world or simulators, and connecting perception, reasoning, and action.

I am happy to explore problems at the intersection of these areas, such as RL-finetuned LLMs, diffusion-based sequence models, and embodied agents driven by language and vision.

Research

Diffusion LLM with Flexible-Length Generation Aug. 2025 – present · with Dr. Yitao Liang, Peking University

We study how fixed-length generation in diffusion-based large language models can degrade generation quality and fail to match real-world requirements where sequence length is inherently variable. I worked on implementing experimental code, running controlled studies, and analyzing the causes of low-quality decoding (such as non-canonical token encodings) under different length-control strategies.

This project has given me a deeper understanding of diffusion models on discrete spaces and token-level probabilistic modeling for diffusion LLMs. A manuscript based on this work is in preparation and planned to be submitted to ICML 2026.

Selected Projects

CUDA-Accelerated Neural Network Framework with Autograd Course project · Programming in AI, 2025

Implemented a small neural network framework from scratch, trained on MNIST using PyTorch as a reference. I implemented core components including Tensor, fully connected and convolutional layers, softmax and cross-entropy loss in CUDA, and built an autograd engine that supports diverse computation graphs.

I also implemented an SGD optimizer and used pybind11 to expose CUDA/C++ kernels to Python, enabling parallelized training and evaluation.

Computer Vision Labs and 3D Understanding Course labs · Introduction to Computer Vision, 2025

Implemented classic feature and edge detection pipelines including Harris Corner Detector and Canny Edge Detector. Built a CNN from scratch with NumPy implementations of convolution, batch normalization, and cross-entropy loss, and trained it on CIFAR-10.

For 3D understanding, I sampled point clouds with uniform and Farthest Point Sampling, performed camera calibration via linear algebra formulations, and experimented with PointNet, Mask R-CNN, and RNNs for 3D perception and sequence modeling tasks.
